Iran's Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei killed in US-Israeli strikes, state media says

By Jon Gambrell
February 28, 2026

DUBAI, United Arab Emirates (AP) - Iran's Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei was killed in U.S.-Israeli airstrikes targeting his compound in Tehran, Iranian state television reported Sunday, marking a dramatic escalation in tensions that threatens to destabilize the Middle East.

State TV described Khamenei's death as "martyrdom" and said the strikes hit while he was inside his official residence in the Iranian capital. There was no immediate independent confirmation of the circumstances of his death.

U.S. President Donald Trump and Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu had earlier suggested that Khamenei may have been killed in the strikes, which they described as aimed at senior Iranian leadership and military targets.

If confirmed, Khamenei's death would end nearly four decades of rule by one of the most powerful figures in Iran's modern history. He became supreme leader in 1989 following the death of Ayatollah Ruhollah Khomeini and oversaw the consolidation of clerical authority, the expansion of Iran's regional influence and repeated crackdowns on domestic dissent.

Under Khamenei, Iran expanded its support for allied militant groups across the Middle East, including Hezbollah in Lebanon, Hamas in the Gaza Strip and the Houthi rebels in Yemen. His leadership was also marked by sustained hostility toward the United States and Israel, as well as continued disputes over Iran's nuclear program.

Iran has faced years of economic pressure from Western sanctions, contributing to periodic waves of protests. Demonstrations in 2009 over disputed presidential elections and in 2022 following the death of Mahsa Amini in morality police custody were met with harsh government crackdowns.

In recent months, renewed unrest over economic hardship and political repression had intensified pressure on the Islamic Republic. Security forces responded forcefully to protests that spread nationwide.

Iran's constitution provides for an interim leadership arrangement in the event of the supreme leader's death, with an Assembly of Experts responsible for appointing a successor. It remained unclear who might emerge as a leading candidate.

Regional leaders reacted cautiously, warning that the development could further inflame an already volatile situation. Oil markets showed immediate signs of instability amid concerns over potential retaliation and broader conflict.

Khamenei, born in 1939 in the city of Mashhad, rose to prominence as a cleric opposed to the rule of Shah Mohammad Reza Pahlavi. After the 1979 Islamic Revolution, he held several key positions before becoming president and later supreme leader.

His tenure shaped Iran's domestic politics and foreign policy for generations. Analysts said his death could usher in a period of significant uncertainty for the country and the broader region.
